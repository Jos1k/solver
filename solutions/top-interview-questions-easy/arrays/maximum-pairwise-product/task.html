Maximum Pairwise Product

Maximum Pairwise Product Problem Find the maximum product of two distinct num- bers in a sequence of non-negative integers. Input: A sequence of non-negative integers. Output: The maximum value that can be obtained by multiplying two different elements from the se- quence. Given a sequence of non-negative integers a1,...,an, compute max ai*aj. Note that i and j should be different, though it may be the case that ai = aj . Input format. The first line contains an integer n. The next line contains n non-negative integers a1,...,an (separated by spaces). Output format. The maximum pairwise product. Constraints. 2 ≤ n ≤ 2 * 10^5; 0≤a1,...,an ≤ 2*10^5. Sample: Input: 1 2 3 Output: 6
